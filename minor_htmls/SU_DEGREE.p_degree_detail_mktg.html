MARKETING MINOR UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAM (MKTG-MINOR)
  Diploma Area Advisor: Vahid Karimi Motahhar

The goal of the Marketing Minor Program is to equip students from diverse academic backgrounds with a practical and contemporary understanding of marketing as a core business function. The program emphasizes how marketing decisions are made in real organizational settings, combining customer insight, digital platforms, data, and strategy. Graduates of the minor develop a marketing mindset that complements their major and enhances their ability to create value in complex, competitive environments.

In today’s business environment, marketing is no longer a standalone function limited to advertising or promotion; it is central to how organizations design products, interact with customers, leverage data, and compete in digital markets. Professionals across disciplines, including engineering, economics, data science, psychology, and management are increasingly expected to understand customer behavior, market dynamics, branding, and data-driven decision making. The Marketing Minor responds to this reality by offering students a structured yet flexible way to acquire applied marketing knowledge that enhances their primary field of study.

The Marketing Minor is structured around a flexible track system that allows students to tailor the program to their interests and career goals. While all students complete a common introductory foundation in marketing, they may shape their pathway by selecting courses aligned with one of three suggested tracks: Consumer & Behavioral, Quantitative & Modeling, or Marketing Strategy. These tracks are not rigid specializations but recommended directions that help students navigate a broad elective pool. Students may follow a single track in depth or combine courses across tracks to build an interdisciplinary profile that best complements their major.

The Marketing Minor is designed for undergraduate students from all disciplines who wish to complement their major with applied marketing knowledge. It is particularly well suited for students interested in working in interdisciplinary teams, engaging with customers and markets, launching new products or services, or applying marketing insights in fields such as technology, sustainability, healthcare, arts analytics, and entrepreneurship. The program is ideal for students who want to strengthen their career readiness by understanding how organizations create value through markets, customers, and data regardless of whether they plan to pursue a marketing-specific role.
